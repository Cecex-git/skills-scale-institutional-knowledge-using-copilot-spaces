# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Leads

### Role Summary
Product Leads oversee product strategy and guide Product Managers in their execution. They align product initiatives with business objectives and ensure portfolio-level coherence.

### Responsibilities
- Set product strategy and vision across multiple product areas
- Guide and mentor Product Managers
- Make prioritization decisions for high-impact features
- Align cross-product dependencies and trade-offs
- Approve project one-pagers and success metrics
- Represent product in executive reviews and planning

### Goals
- Maximize product portfolio value and market impact
- Ensure strategic alignment across product initiatives
- Enable Product Managers to deliver effectively
- Maintain competitive advantage and innovation

### Typical Communication
- Weekly 1:1s with Product Managers
- Monthly executive business reviews
- Quarterly roadmap planning sessions
- Ad-hoc strategic decisions and escalation resolution

---

## Sponsors

### Role Summary
Sponsors are senior stakeholders who provide executive support, resources, and decision-making authority for strategic projects. They champion initiatives and remove organizational barriers.

### Responsibilities
- Provide executive approval and funding for projects
- Remove organizational blockers and escalations
- Make go/no-go decisions at key project gates
- Ensure alignment with business strategy and priorities
- Communicate project value to leadership
- Authorize resource allocation and budget

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value
- Enable teams to execute effectively
- Manage organizational change and adoption
- Maintain stakeholder confidence and support

### Typical Communication
- Monthly project status reviews
- Gate decision meetings (initiation, planning approval)
- Escalation resolution as needed
- Executive briefings and board updates

---

## QA/Testing Specialists

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Specialists ensure product quality through test planning, execution, and validation. They work closely with developers to define acceptance criteria and verify features meet quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Create comprehensive test plans and test cases
- Execute manual and automated testing
- Validate acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Identify and document defects
- Collaborate on test automation strategy
- Participate in requirement reviews to ensure testability
- Sign off on feature readiness for release

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high product quality and reliability
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Enable faster, safer releases

### Typical Communication
- Daily collaboration with developers on features
- Test readiness reviews before release
- Bug triage and prioritization meetings
- Sprint planning to estimate testing effort

---

## Security Engineers

### Role Summary
Security Engineers protect systems and data by identifying vulnerabilities, implementing security controls, and guiding secure development practices. They provide security expertise throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Review code and architecture for security vulnerabilities
- Configure and monitor security scanning tools
- Triage security findings and recommend remediation
- Define security requirements and best practices
- Respond to security incidents
- Conduct threat modeling for new features
- Maintain security documentation and runbooks

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities in production
- Minimize attack surface and security risk
- Enable secure development practices
- Ensure compliance with security standards

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews during design and code review
- Weekly security scan triage meetings
- Incident response coordination
- Security training and awareness sessions
- On-call rotation for security incidents
